Leadership & Influence

Whether steering a team or an initiative, I drive outcomes

Problem-solving rigor, visibility, and ownership — applied whether I’m leading people or leading the work itself.

🙌 I write the docs I lead

I walk the talk. Beyond managing, I personally own features and products end-to-end as the writer — user guides, API references, and whatever content the feature demands. I stay close to the craft and the product.

🧮 Decisions grounded in data

I base direction on data, not opinion — using it to define and conclude my and my team’s action items, prioritize effort, and prove whether the work actually moved the needle.

📣 Communicate up, consistently

Structured weekly updates to leadership — initiative status, contributor-level data, blockers, next steps — making the team’s work legible without being asked.

🤝 Delegate ownership, not tasks

Assign end-to-end ownership of major initiatives and credit each person to leadership, building their visibility and growth.

🎯 Coach before the moment

Prep team members with structured talking points before key meetings — developing presence and confidence.

📈 Make performance visible

Track contribution and docs performance every month, comparing month-over-month and quarter-over-quarter trends to decide concrete actions for the next cycle — proving impact with numbers, not assertions.

🔄 Pivot fast on bad bets

Diagnose root cause, document what works, and propose a better workflow instead of pushing a failing path.

🌱 Define growth paths

Five growth axes for the team: analytics & strategy, AI & automation, technical enablement, documentation architecture, and mentorship.

🛡️ Advocate for the team

Escalate real workflow friction to tooling/IT leadership with a clear business case — advocating for conditions, not just output.

🔬 Fix problems at the root

When contributions get rejected, I dig into why — finding the real cause (unclear guidelines, gaps in the process) and fixing it at the source, so fewer get rejected and less effort is wasted on rework.
